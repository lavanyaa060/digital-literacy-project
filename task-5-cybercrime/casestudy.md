# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime in which attackers try to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. This is usually done through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted sources like banks or companies.

A typical phishing attack begins when a user receives an email or SMS claiming to be from a bank or service provider. The message often contains a link and urges the user to take immediate action, such as updating account details or verifying information. When the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks identical to the original. The user then enters their login credentials or banking details, which are captured by the attacker.

Phishing attacks usually target students, working professionals, and elderly people who may not be aware of such scams. The consequences can be severe, including financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting with unknown links and messages online.
